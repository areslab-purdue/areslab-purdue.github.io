Building the Future of Autonomous Transportation

We are the ARES (Autonomous Research and Engineering Systems) Lab at Purdue University's Lyles School of Civil Engineering. Our research focuses on developing next-generation autonomous vehicle systems through advanced robotics, computer vision, machine learning, and vehicle dynamics. We tackle critical challenges in autonomous navigation, perception, and control.

Research Areas

Our research spans multiple interconnected domains in autonomous vehicle technology, from perception and planning to control and safety.

Perception & Sensing

Developing advanced computer vision and LiDAR-based perception systems for robust environment understanding, object detection, and semantic segmentation in autonomous vehicles.

SLAM & Localization

Designing intelligent path planning algorithms and control systems including MPC, trajectory optimization, and vehicle dynamics for safe autonomous navigation.

Our Team

Principal Investigator

Dr. Samuel Labi

Professor, Lyles School of Civil Engineering

Dr. Labi is a professor of transportation and infrastructure systems engineering at Purdue University's Lyles School of Civil Engineering. He is the director of NEXTRANS (Next Generation Transportation Systems) and associate director of CCAT (Center for Connected and Automated Transportation). He received his PhD from Purdue University in 2001 and has published over 100 scientific articles and authored two textbooks used in universities worldwide. His research focuses on transportation systems, infrastructure management, connected and autonomous vehicles, and transportation economics.

AMP - Autonomous Mobility Purdue

Autonomous Mobility Purdue (AMP) is a student-led undergraduate team dedicated to advancing autonomous vehicle technology through hands-on research and competition. The team designs, builds, and tests autonomous ground vehicles, focusing on perception, navigation, and control systems. AMP provides students with real-world experience in robotics, software engineering, and system integration while competing in national autonomous vehicle challenges.
